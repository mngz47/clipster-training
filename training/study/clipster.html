Catchphrases to look for?

Viral Video Editing Format
To maximize your chances of going viral, you’ll want to follow a proven editing format that works across platforms like TikTok and Instagram Reels. These platforms are built to promote content that’s fast-paced, emotionally engaging, and easy to consume — so your editing should reflect that.
Here’s a step-by-step breakdown of the ideal viral video editing format:
1. Start with a Hook (First 1–3 Seconds):

The beginning of your video is the most critical. Viewers decide within seconds whether to keep watching. Use a bold visual, a surprising moment, or a strong opening phrase like:
“POV: You finally proved everyone wrong.”
2. Add a Catchy Phrase or Caption:

Overlay text that adds context or emotion to the clip. It could be motivational, humorous, relatable, or even dramatic — depending on the tone of the video. Phrases like:
“He risked everything for this one moment,”
or
“From \$0 to 10K — this is how it started,”
create curiosity and encourage viewers to watch till the end.
3. Incorporate Trending Audio or Sound Effects:

Use viral or trending sounds that are currently performing well on TikTok or Instagram. Audio helps build emotion and drives shareability. You can find trending sounds directly on TikTok's or Instagram’s audio library or by browsing their "Trending" sections.
4. Keep It Short and Punchy (10–30 Seconds):

Don’t over-edit. Keep the pace quick and cut out any dull moments. Every second should add value — visually, emotionally, or narratively.
5. Use Clear Visuals and High Contrast:

Make sure the footage is clean, easy to follow, and looks good on mobile screens. Add slight zooms, slow-motion, or quick cuts if needed to maintain interest.
6. Include Branding or Logo (If Applicable):

If you're posting for a campaign, subtly include your logo or brand name without distracting from the content. This could be in a corner, on a prop, or woven into the story naturally.
7. Post on TikTok and Instagram Reels:

These are the best platforms for discoverability and viral reach. Be sure to use relevant hashtags, post at active times (evenings and weekends typically work best), and consider reposting top-performing clips with slight variations.
